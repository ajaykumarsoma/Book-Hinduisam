    Physical Description from Ancient Sources¶

    Encyclopedia Britannica on Hermae:

    "Pillars that were generally square and tapering toward the bottom so as to suggest the human figure. These were usually surmounted by the bearded head of Hermes (hence the name) and had an erect phallus. They were used not only as cult objects but also for a variety of other purposes, for example, as milestones or boundary marks. They were regarded with respect, if not actually worshipped."

    Oxford Classical Dictionary:

    "A stone pillar marking crossroads and boundaries. It is topped with a head, usually of Hermes or Dionysos, with an upright phallus carved at the front, and squared shoulders over which wreathes or clothes could be draped."

### **Das Kapital: Logical Fallacies and Economic Nonsense**

**Marx's magnum opus is built on a foundation of logical fallacies that economists have refuted for over a century.**

#### **Fallacy #1: The Labor Theory of Value (LTV)**

**Marx's Claim:**

> "The value of a commodity is determined by the **socially necessary labor time** required to produce it."

**The Refutation:**

**Eugen von Böhm-Bawerk** (Austrian economist, 1896):

> "Marx's labor theory of value is logically incoherent. If value comes only from labor, then why do diamonds (little labor) cost more than water (abundant but laborious to transport)? **Subjective utility, not labor time, determines value.**"
> — **Karl Marx and the Close of His System** (1896)

**Modern Consensus:**

> "The Labor Theory of Value faces the problem of **joint production**: if a production process produces more than one commodity, how does one calculate socially necessary labor time? Some labor values can be **undefined, nil, or negative**."
> — **Ian Steedman, economist** (cited in multiple academic sources)

> "**Modern fiat money utterly refutes Marx's labor theory of value**, because money needs to be a produced commodity with a labor value for his theory to work. But money has long since ceased to be a produced commodity and is now fiat money."
> — **Social Democracy for the 21st Century blog**

**Why This Matters:**

**Without the LTV, Marx's entire theory of "exploitation" collapses.** If labor doesn't create all value, then profit isn't "stolen surplus value" — it's compensation for capital, risk, and entrepreneurship.

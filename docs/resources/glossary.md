# Sanskrit Glossary

A comprehensive glossary of Sanskrit terms used throughout the book.

---

## A

**Ā** bhiṣeka (अभिषेक)  
Sacred bathing or anointment ritual; pouring of sacred substances (water, milk, honey) over a deity

**Āgama** (आगम)  
Texts dealing with temple worship, iconography, and ritual; distinct from Vedas

**Ātman** (आत्मन्)  
The individual soul or self; considered identical with Brahman in Advaita Vedanta

**Āvāhana** (आवाहन)  
Invocation; ritual of inviting a deity to enter a murti or sacred space

---

## B

**Bhakti** (भक्ति)  
Devotion; loving devotion to a personal deity as a path to liberation

**Brahman** (ब्रह्मन्)  
The ultimate reality; the absolute, unchanging cosmic principle in Vedānta

---

## D

**Darśana** (दर्शन)  
Lit. "seeing"; auspicious sight of a deity, saint, or sacred object; also refers to philosophical systems

**Devī** (देवी)  
Goddess; the divine feminine principle

**Dharma** (धर्म)  
Righteousness, duty, cosmic law, natural order; one of the four purusharthas

---

## G

**Garbhagṛha** (गर्भगृह)  
Lit. "womb-chamber"; the sanctum sanctorum of a temple where the main deity resides

**Gopuram** (गोपुरम्)  
Monumental entrance tower of South Indian temples

---

## H

**Homa** (होम)  
Fire ritual; offering oblations into sacred fire (also called havan, yajña)

---

## K

**Karma** (कर्मन्)  
Action; the universal law of cause and effect; accumulated consequences of actions

**Kumkuma** (कुङ्कुम)  
Red vermillion powder used in Hindu rituals

---

## L

**Liṅga** (लिङ्ग)  
Symbol of Śiva; cylindrical stone form representing the formless absolute

---

## M

**Mantra** (मन्त्र)  
Sacred sound, syllable, or phrase with spiritual power; used in meditation and ritual

**Mokṣa** (मोक्ष)  
Liberation from the cycle of birth and death; ultimate spiritual goal

**Mūrti** (मूर्ति)  
Form, image, embodiment; consecrated deity statue in temple worship

---

## N

**Netronmīlana** (नेत्रोन्मीलन)  
Lit. "eye-opening"; the ritual moment when a murti's eyes are symbolically opened during consecration

**Nyāsa** (न्यास)  
Ritual placement; touching parts of the body or murti while chanting mantras to install divine energies

---

## P

**Pañcaloha** (पञ्चलोह)  
Five-metal alloy (gold, silver, copper, brass, iron) used for making murtis

**Pañcāmṛta** (पञ्चामृत)  
Five nectars: milk, yogurt, ghee, honey, sugar; used in abhisheka rituals

**Prāṇa** (प्राण)  
Life-force, vital breath, vital energy; one of the five vāyus

**Prāṇa Pratiṣṭhā** (प्राण प्रतिष्ठा)  
Lit. "establishment of life-force"; consecration ritual to install divine consciousness into a murti

**Pūjā** (पूजा)  
Worship; ritual of honoring a deity with offerings, prayers, and devotion

**Purāṇa** (पुराण)  
Ancient texts containing mythology, cosmology, genealogies, and religious practices

---

## S

**Śakti** (शक्ति)  
Power, energy; the divine feminine creative force

**Saṅkalpa** (सङ्कल्प)  
Focused intention, vow, mental resolve; declaration of purpose in rituals

**Saṃsāra** (संसार)  
The cycle of birth, death, and rebirth

**Sanātana Dharma** (सनातन धर्म)  
Lit. "eternal order/law"; traditional term for Hinduism

**Śāstra** (शास्त्र)  
Scripture, treatise; authoritative text on a particular subject

**Śilpa Śāstra** (शिल्प शास्त्र)  
Texts on iconography, sculpture, temple architecture, and sacred proportions

**Svayambhū** (स्वयम्भू)  
Self-manifested; deity forms believed to have appeared naturally, not carved by humans

---

## U

**Upaniṣad** (उपनिषद्)  
Philosophical texts forming the end portion of the Vedas; focus on Brahman and Ātman

---

## V

**Vāstu** (वास्तु)  
Architecture; science of sacred space and building design

**Veda** (वेद)  
Lit. "knowledge"; the four ancient sacred texts (Ṛg, Yajur, Sāma, Atharva)

**Viṣṇu** (विष्णु)  
The preserver deity in the Trimūrti; worshipped in various avatāras

---

## Y

**Yajña** (यज्ञ)  
Vedic fire sacrifice; ritual offering to deities

**Yantra** (यन्त्र)  
Sacred geometric diagram used in worship and meditation
